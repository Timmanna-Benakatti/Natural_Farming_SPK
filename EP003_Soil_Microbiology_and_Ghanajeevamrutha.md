# Episode 3 – Soil Microbiology and Ghanajeevamrutha

## Executive Summary

This episode explains the biological foundation of natural farming, emphasizing soil microorganisms, native soil, nitrogen fixation, biodiversity, and Ghanajeevamrutha. It reinforces that healthy soils depend on living microbial communities rather than external chemical inputs.

## Learning Objectives

- Understand soil microbial diversity.
- Learn the role of native soil.
- Understand nitrogen fixation.
- Learn handling of Ghanajeevamrutha.

## Major Topics

- Soil microorganisms
- Rhizobium and Frankia
- Soil pH
- Jeevamrutha refinement
- Ghanajeevamrutha
- Biodiversity

## Practical Recommendations

### Recommended

- Use indigenous cow inputs.
- Collect soil from chemical-free locations.
- Promote biodiversity.
- Rotate crops.

### Avoid

- Plastic-sealed fermentation.
- Continuous monocropping.
- Over-drying Ghanajeevamrutha.

## Key Takeaways

- Healthy soil is a living ecosystem.
- Native microorganisms are valuable.
- Biological nitrogen fixation reduces external inputs.
- Biodiversity improves resilience.
