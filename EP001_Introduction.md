# What is Natural Farming?

Natural farming means understanding the natural processes that already exist in forests and applying the same principles in agricultural land.

Key ideas:

- Nature grows forests without fertilizers.
- Nature grows forests without pesticides.
- Nature maintains soil fertility automatically.
- Humans should support nature instead of controlling it.

# Core Philosophy

> Everything required by the plant already exists inside the soil.

The farmer's responsibility is NOT to feed the plant.

The farmer's responsibility is to activate the biological processes that make nutrients available.

---

# Important Principle 1
## Soil Already Contains Nutrients

Common misconception:

> Plants need fertilizers.

Natural farming view:

The soil already contains nearly all required nutrients.

Microorganisms convert unavailable nutrients into plant-available nutrients.

---

# Important Principle 2
## Sunlight

The speaker considers sunlight the first input.

Different crops require different sunlight intensity.

Examples:

- Cereals need full sunlight.
- Vegetables require partial shade.
- Multi-layer cropping improves sunlight utilization.

---

# Important Principle 3
## Water

One of the strongest messages:

Plants do not "drink" water the way humans do.

Instead:

- Root hairs absorb moisture.
- Atmospheric humidity is important.
- Excess irrigation creates disease.

Incorrect practice:

Watering directly at the trunk.

Correct practice:

Provide moisture where feeder roots exist (around the canopy drip line).

---

# Important Principle 4
## Mixed Cropping

Recommended:

- Monocot + Dicot
- Crop rotation
- Multi-cropping

Avoid:

- Continuous monocropping

Benefits:

- Better nutrient cycling
- Improved soil biology
- Reduced pest pressure

---

# Role of Forests

Forests prove that:

- No fertilizers are required.
- No pesticides are required.
- No irrigation systems are required.
- Nature sustains itself.

Agriculture should imitate forest ecology.

---

# Farmer Mindset

Natural farming is NOT:

- A fertilizer recipe
- A pesticide replacement
- A farming product

Natural farming IS:

- Knowledge
- Observation
- Ecology
- Understanding natural processes

# Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Zero Budget Natural Farming?

A farming system that minimizes external inputs by relying on natural ecological processes.

### Why is it called Zero Budget?

Because dependency on purchased fertilizers and chemicals is minimized.

### Does soil contain nutrients?

Yes. Natural farming believes nutrients already exist and microorganisms make them available.

### Is more irrigation better?

No. Excess irrigation encourages diseases.

### Why imitate forests?

Forests maintain fertility naturally without external inputs.
