# EP002 - Natural Farming Episode 2

> Source: YouTube transcript (Kannada)

## Executive Summary

This episode focuses on **Jeevamrutha**, one of the most important microbial formulations used in Subhash Palekar Natural Farming (SPNF). The speaker explains that Jeevamrutha is **not a fertilizer**, but a microbial inoculant that increases beneficial microorganisms in the soil. These microorganisms convert nutrients already present in the soil into forms that plants can absorb.

The episode also covers:
- Ingredients required for Jeevamrutha
- Step-by-step preparation
- Fermentation process
- Application methods
- Frequency of application
- Importance of indigenous cow dung and urine
- Role of leguminous crops in nitrogen fixation
- Benefits of intercropping and biodiversity
- Motivation for chemical-free farming and encouraging youth participation.

## Learning Objectives

- Understand the purpose of Jeevamrutha.
- Learn the preparation process.
- Learn recommended application methods.
- Understand why soil biology is central to natural farming.
- Learn why intercropping with legumes is encouraged.

## Jeevamrutha Recipe

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|------------|----------|
| Indigenous cow dung | 5–10 kg |
| Indigenous cow urine | 5–10 L |
| Pulse flour (non-oilseed) | 2 kg |
| Jaggery | 2 kg |
| Healthy soil | One handful |
| Water | 200 L |

## Preparation

1. Fill a 200 L drum with water.
2. Add all ingredients.
3. Stir clockwise thoroughly.
4. Stir 2–3 times daily.
5. Ferment 2–7 days in summer or up to 10 days in cooler weather.

## Application

- Drip irrigation (filtered)
- Sprinkler
- Soil drenching

### Frequency

- Short duration crops: twice during crop cycle.
- Long duration crops: once every month.

## Important Concepts

### Jeevamrutha is NOT fertilizer

Its purpose is to multiply microorganisms.

### Soil already contains nutrients

Microorganisms convert unavailable nutrients into plant-available nutrients.

### Nitrogen fixation

Grow legumes and nitrogen-fixing trees such as Gliricidia, Agase and Moringa along with main crops.

### Intercropping

Recommended combinations:

- Monocot + Dicot
- Onion → Groundnut
- Onion → Chickpea

Benefits include biodiversity, nitrogen fixation and lower cultivation costs.

## Common Mistakes

- Using hybrid cow dung.
- Using oilseed flour.
- Continuous monocropping.
- Ignoring biodiversity.

## Key Takeaways

- Use indigenous cow products.
- Promote soil microorganisms.
- Encourage intercropping.
- Reduce chemical dependency.
- Improve soil fertility naturally.

## Keywords

Jeevamrutha, SPNF, Soil Biology, Indigenous Cow, Nitrogen Fixation, Intercropping, Natural Farming
